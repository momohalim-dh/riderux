A Q3 update

Designing around
priorities

Moving from permanent squad assignment to priority-driven allocation — so design effort lands where it matters most.

The shift

Before vs. Q3 approach

⬤ Before

Permanent squad assignment

Designers are locked to a single squad, regardless of what priorities look like across the domain or tribe.

  • Capacity doesn't follow impact
  • High-priority initiatives can be under-resourced
  • Almost no tribe-level prioritization in practice
  • Limited exposure stagnates growth after a point
⬤ Q3 Approach

Priority-driven allocation

Designers default to their squad, but capacity can shift to higher-impact work at domain or tribe level when needed.

  • Allocation follows the highest-priority work
  • Tribe-level view surfaces cross-domain needs
  • Squads still get their designer as a baseline
  • Flexibility without losing predictability
Q Planning process

Three steps, same planning day

The overall Q planning approach stays the same — these are minor, targeted adjustments.

1
Define squad-level priorities
📋 Squad + Designer

On planning day, each squad defines and ranks their design initiatives. This gives us a clear starting point — the designer's default assignment is their squad, and squad-level work is the baseline expectation.

2
Rank priorities at domain & tribe level
🤝 Design Manager + GPMs → Momo + Tribe Leader → Fabian

Design initiatives across all squads are reviewed jointly by the Design Manager and GPMs to establish domain-level priorities.


Once domain priorities are set, they're reviewed by Momo and the Tribe Leader to determine tribe-level priorities, then aligned with Fabian. If there are any conflicts at the tribe level, Fabian is the final decision maker.

3
Allocate designers to top priorities
📐 Design Lead

Starting from the tribe-level priority list, designers are assigned beginning with their default squad. If a higher-impact initiative exists elsewhere and the squad's designer is at capacity, another designer steps in. This cascades from domain to tribe level as needed.

Allocation logic

How a designer gets assigned

Priority cascade — squad → domain → tribe
🎯
Squad level · Default

Designer assigned to their home squad

Every designer starts here. Squad work is the baseline — designers own their squad's design initiatives unless capacity needs rebalancing.

⚡
Domain level · When needed

Higher-impact initiative, no squad designer capacity

If a top-ranked initiative in the domain can't be covered by that squad's designer (already at capacity), another designer within the domain steps in to take it over.

🌐
Tribe level · When needed

High-priority initiative crossing domains

If the highest-priority work sits across domains and no squad designer has capacity, allocation is applied at the tribe level — moving a designer across domain boundaries to cover the most impactful work.

💡

What this isn't

This isn't a complete restructure. Designers still have a home squad and default assignment. The flexibility only kicks in when priorities clearly warrant it — and only after being validated at domain and tribe level with GPMs and Momo.
